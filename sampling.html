üìö Understanding Sampling & Confidence

üéØ What is Bootstrap Sampling?

Bootstrap is a resampling technique where we repeatedly sample from our own data WITH replacement to estimate uncertainty.

Resample size = Original sample size

Why it works: By resampling, we simulate "what if we collected data again?" This shows us how much our statistics might vary.

üìè Confidence Intervals

A 95% confidence interval gives a range where we're 95% confident the true population parameter lies.

95% CI = Mean ¬± (1.96 √ó SE)

Interpretation: "We're 95% confident the true mean is between [lower] and [upper]."

‚ö†Ô∏è Not: "There's a 95% probability the mean is in this range." (The mean is fixed; our interval is what varies)

üìà Law of Large Numbers

As sample size increases, the sample mean gets closer to the true population mean.

  • Small samples: High uncertainty, variable estimates
  • Large samples: Low uncertainty, stable estimates
  • Diminishing returns: Each additional sample helps less

üåç Geography Applications

Survey research: Report CIs to show uncertainty in estimates

Field sampling: Determine how many samples needed for desired precision

Polling: "Margin of error" is the same as confidence interval half-width

üí° Practical Tips

  • Always report confidence intervals, not just point estimates
  • Bigger samples = narrower CIs = more precision
  • Bootstrap works even when data isn't normal
  • Consider cost-benefit: Is 4√ó sample size worth 2√ó precision?
